# Funding Analysis: EU and Dutch Grant Opportunities for TARIDE

**Date:** 2026-04-06

---

## Executive Summary

There are real, accessible funding opportunities for TARIDE — but the timing varies dramatically. The most aligned EU calls (Digital Europe trust/identity) have already closed. The best near-term opportunities are Dutch and smaller EU instruments. The large Horizon Europe calls require consortium partners and 5+ months of preparation.

**Key finding:** A Dutch non-profit stichting qualifies for **100% funding** under Horizon Europe (all action types). This is a significant structural advantage over for-profit applicants who receive only 70% for Innovation Actions.

**Critical prerequisite:** The stichting must be formally incorporated (KvK registered) before any EU application can proceed. This is a hard blocker.

---

## Is This the Right Moment?

**Yes — but not for the reasons expected.**

The timing is right not because there's a perfect open call waiting, but because:

1. **The regulatory window is open.** eIDAS 2.0 implementation is active (2024-2027). The Dutch EDI Wallet programme has EUR 15M+ budget. The Digital Commons EDIC launched in The Hague with the Netherlands as founding member. EU policy is actively looking for projects like TARIDE.

2. **The next funding cycle is being shaped now.** The Digital Europe work programme was amended in October 2025 and March 2026, adding EUDI Wallet support and Digital Commons actions. The EU Open Source Strategy is expected in 2026 (call for evidence opened January 2026). Engaging now positions TARIDE for calls that haven't been published yet.

3. **The most directly relevant EU call already closed.** DIGITAL-2024-BESTUSE-TECH-06-TRUST (European Digital Identity and Trust Ecosystem, EUR 20M) closed September 2024. A successor call under the 2025-2027 work programme is likely but unconfirmed. Missing this cycle means the next window is H2 2026 at earliest.

4. **Smaller Dutch instruments are available now** and can fund the PoC phase while larger applications are prepared.

**The risk of waiting:** CAMARA, Apple/Google, and eIDAS wallet implementations are advancing. Every quarter without external validation (funding, partnerships) makes the competitive positioning harder.

## What Does It Take?

### Prerequisites (all programmes)

1. **Incorporate the stichting.** Hard blocker for any EU application. Register at KvK, obtain PIC on EU Funding & Tenders Portal. Timeline: 2-4 weeks.
2. **Resolve the foundation document's blockers.** Grant evaluators will read the technical description. The gaps identified in the reviews (no threat model, no DID method selection, no financial model) weaken any application. At minimum, Tier 1 items from the consolidation must be addressed.

### Per-programme requirements

| Programme | Consortium | Co-funding | Key challenge |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| NLnet | Single applicant OK | 0% | Competitive but accessible |
| SIDN | Single applicant OK | 0% | Small amount — useful for validation only |
| MIT | Single or partnership | 65% own contribution | Must demonstrate economic activity |
| WBSO | Single applicant | N/A (tax credit) | Must have employees on payroll |
| NWO | Academic lead + partners | 0% (NWO funds 100%) | Finding academic partner willing to lead |
| CIF-NL | Single or consortium | ~50% | Cybersecurity framing required |
| Horizon Europe | Min 3 from 3 EU countries | 0% (100% for non-profits) | Consortium building, 50-100 page proposal |
| Digital Europe | Min 3 from 3 EU countries | 50% co-funding | Co-funding is the hard constraint |

### Timeline reality

## Recommended Strategy

### Phase 1: Quick wins (April-June 2026)

1. **Incorporate the stichting immediately** — prerequisite for everything
2. **Apply to NLnet NGI Zero Commons Fund** (deadline 1 June) — highest alignment, low effort, no consortium needed
3. **Apply to SIDN Fonds Pioneer** (rolling) — small but fast validation
4. **Register for WBSO** as soon as first employee is on payroll

### Phase 2: Build consortium (May-September 2026)

5. **Identify academic partner** for NWO Resilient Digital Ecosystems (deadline 15 September)
6. **Begin consortium outreach** for Horizon CL3 ECCC-03 (deadline 15 September) — need partners in 2 other EU countries with relevant expertise (post-quantum crypto, DID systems, telecom security)
7. **Apply to CIF-NL** when next round opens (~Q4 2026)

### Phase 3: Strategic positioning (H2 2026 onward)

8. **Engage Digital Commons EDIC** — Netherlands is founding member
9. **Submit input to EU Open Source Strategy** consultation
10. **Engage Dutch EDI Wallet programme** at EZK
11. **Monitor for next DIGITAL BESTUSE-TECH-TRUST call** — prepare consortium in advance
12. **Prepare Horizon Cluster 4 2027 application** (Open Internet Stack) — the big one

### Parallel: Don't depend on grants

Grants are a strategic accelerant, not a survival mechanism. Per the cost review (COST-006), relying on grants as primary funding is high-risk (15-17% success rate for Horizon, 12-18 month timelines). The funding strategy must include non-grant sources:

- Calmido B.V. operational funding for Foundation bootstrap
- Telecom partner co-development investment
- Philanthropic funding (digital rights foundations)
- Revenue from protocol fees (long-term)
